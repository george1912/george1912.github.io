Project Apollo

Healthcare is care.
Organization is how we deliver it.

This project is a personal system and portfolio expression. It documents how I turn nursing school intensity into repeatable structure, better decisions, and more confident clinical execution.

Chapter 01
Why this exists

Not just healthcare. A system for showing up prepared.

I am building Apollo as a living operations model: one place for study plans, skills progression, and reflection loops. The aim is consistency under pressure, with tools that make each week easier to execute than the last.

Care Lens

  • Patient safety starts with personal readiness.
  • Clarity reduces avoidable errors.
  • Reflection drives better interventions.

Organization Lens

  • Weekly planning before daily execution.
  • Evidence over mood for studying priorities.
  • Small systems outperform bursts of effort.
Chapter 02
What Apollo builds

One private hub. Multiple workflows.

Apollo combines a study OS, skill checkoff confidence tracking, and a structured clinical reflection archive. Together they create a narrative of progress instead of scattered notes.

Active Workstreams

  • Med-surg topic planner with impact ranking.
  • Skills checkoff tracker with confidence trend.
  • Rotation notes template with post-shift prompts.

In Development

  • Question-bank rehearsal rhythm by weak category.
  • Exam sprint dashboard for seven-day windows.
  • Weekly review scorecard and bottleneck log.
Chapter 03
Milestones

Build cadence from now through clinical rotations.

Apollo is built in phases: stabilize daily workflow, improve exam performance, and ship a reusable operating model that can evolve with each semester.

Phase 1 Weekly command center and topic prioritization.
Phase 2 Skills confidence trend and prep loop before checkoffs.
Phase 3 Clinical reflection engine with intervention patterns.
Phase 4 Portfolio-ready showcase of system thinking in healthcare.

Project Apollo is self-expression through execution.

The goal is simple: show how design, discipline, and empathy can live in one system. This is where I document the build, sharpen the process, and turn learning into care-ready action.
