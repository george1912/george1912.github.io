"To Kill a Nightingale: My Nusing School Experience"

Objective

As a former marketing developer with a love for nursing and education, my goal is simple. I wish to highlight my student experience and highlight potential areas of improvement within nursing education, not to threaten or cast blame, but to foster dialogue and positive change.

About Me

My name is George Ulloa, and I am a former student in the Accelerated Bachelor of Science in Nursing (ABSN) program at the University at Buffalo (UB). I created this website to share my personal experience and insights about my time at UB, specifically within the School of Nursing.

From the excitement of embarking on my nursing career to the challenges I faced during my academic journey, I encountered a range of experiences that both inspired and tested me. My intention in sharing this story is not merely to recount my own journey, but to highlight certain systematic issues within the program that might resonate with others in similar situations.

The reason behind this site goes beyond my own narrative. It serves as an open letter to those who are invested in the educational system, urging a fair and equitable environment that nurtures talents, recognizes hard work, and values diversity. By bringing my story to light, I hope to contribute to a broader conversation about academic fairness and integrity, with the ultimate goal of fostering positive change in our educational institutions.

Through this platform, you will find my detailed account of events, supporting documents, and reflections on my journey at UB. I invite you to explore and engage with the content, and hope that it will spark dialogue, understanding, and, hopefully, transformation.

Accelerated Issues

The University at Buffalo School of Nursing's ABSN program's unfair dismissal led me to address significant issues within the program. The concerns I raised were:

  • Academic Concerns: Lack of accommodations, unrealistic workloads, teaching inconsistencies, and overall course failure.
  • Behavioral and Social Concerns: Inaction against harassment, alienation by staff, and unfair dismissals that impact mainily students of color.
  • Broader Implications: Contributing to the nursing shortage, causing personal and financial damage.

I believe in the value of nursing education and took an oath to maintain and elevate the standard of my profession. It is my hope that these issues can be acknowledged, addressed, and resolved to provide a fair and equitable nursing program for future students.

UB's Response

I wish to address concerns stemming from comments made by Jennifer Schreier. It came to my attention that Jennifer had formed a perception that I misunderstood the conditions of my probation. This belief emerged from the observation that I hadn't registered for fall classes, an observation even campus security seemed to share. However, I must emphasize the clarity of Jennifer's communication to me:

  • I was informed that I had been removed from both the summer session II and fall classes.
  • The correspondence I received clearly expressed an aspiration for me to return the subsequent summer to retake two key courses: NSG312 and NSG313. The key thing being NEXT summer.
  • Further cementing the aforementioned directives, I was instructed to fill out a Leave of Absence form, ensuring that my enrollment slot was reserved for the upcoming summer.
This incident underscores the inconsistencies and ambiguities I've unfortunately encountered within the ABSN program. More concerning is why say one thing, then tell me another?
